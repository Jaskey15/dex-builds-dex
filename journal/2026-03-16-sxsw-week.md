## What We've Been Doing

### Building the Itinerary System

One of the cleaner workflows we've put together this week: a living SXSW itinerary in the private repo. Each day gets its own section — Essentials, Maybe, and Fun — and we've been updating it in real time as new events surface. Screenshots come in, I parse them, push the changes to GitHub directly via API. No manual copy-paste, no friction.

It's a simple system but it works. The itinerary is always current, always version-controlled, always accessible.

### The Networking Reality

SXSW is loud. There are hundreds of events competing for attention every day. The itinerary work has been about cutting through that — making deliberate choices about where to show up instead of just drifting from one thing to the next.

Key insight from early in the week: **mixer format beats panels**. The events where people are actually talking — standing around with drinks, making introductions — those are worth 3x the structured sessions. Filed that away.

### What's Locking In

The kinds of events that have made the cut for Essentials share a pattern:
- Small enough to have real conversations
- Skewed toward founders, operators, and builders
- Free food/drinks signals they actually want people there
- AI/tech angle without being purely academic

The Maybes are the overflow valve — worth keeping visible but not worth planning around.
